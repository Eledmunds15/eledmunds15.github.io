About

Engineer, Scientist, Builder.

I'm a materials scientist working at the intersection of nuclear energy, advanced materials, and computational modelling. My PhD at Sheffield, jointly funded by the EPSRC CDT in Fusion Power and the UK Atomic Energy Authority, focuses on how irradiation damage governs the structural performance of fusion reactor components.

Before materials, I studied Aerospace Engineering at Sheffield — graduating with a First Class degree, specialising in aeromechanics. My undergraduate research on machine learning classification of defects in additively manufactured metals was published in Materials Science and Technology.

I've done two stints at AtkinsRéalis working on nuclear facility design, structural analysis, and virtual site access. I've also worked in software in Singapore, and led engineering teams on student rocketry and aircraft projects.

Alongside my PhD, I'm laying the foundations of Edmunds — built around a simple belief: we can do better.

Research & Projects

Research, Ventures & Projects.

Active · PhD University of Sheffield & UKAEA

Dislocation Mobility in the Presence of Abundant Point Defects

Simulating how irradiation damage affects creep performance in fusion reactor structural components. Spans atomistic simulations, mesoscale modelling, and crystal plasticity — connecting nanoscale physics to real-world structural behaviour. Supervised by Prof. Christopher Race, Prof. Hector Basoalto-Ibarra, and Dr. Chris Hardie (UKAEA).

Published · 2024 Materials Science & Technology

Classification of Microstructural Defects in Additively Manufactured Aerospace Metals using Machine Learning

Machine learning techniques applied to identify and classify internal defects in additively manufactured metal components — with direct implications for structural integrity assurance in aerospace and high-performance manufacturing.
